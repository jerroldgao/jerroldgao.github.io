##Education Reflection
The education in Iowa State University is driving me to learn more things from outside resources and filling my urge of solving challenge problems.
The most exciting in Iowa State is that I find the path I could donate my passion to. I was an applied physics students become I transferred to computer engineering. Though I felt achievement from solving equation, deducting theory and comfirming correctness of theory in lab, I could not certain if I could continue to do this in future career. My advisor asked me to do self-discovery. After struggling for several months, I finally realized that what I care about is saving time and work by optimizing process. The ultramate time saving tool is no doubt – computer. So I tranferred to computer engineering and chased time.
However, computer engineering is a large and broad area just like an ocean which can get you easily drowned. To get ahead, I seeked for more resources to look at and guides from professors. Fortunately, due to my lab assistant experience in Iowa State MEMS lab, I got enough research skill to search the information I want. I pooled high quality paper, interesting projects and creditable developers’ blogs into my own database prepared for more challenge problems.
